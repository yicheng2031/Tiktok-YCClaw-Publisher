This Privacy Policy explains how YCClaw Publisher (the “Service”) operated by YC Studio (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, shares, and protects information when you use the Service.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account information (e.g., email, name) if you create an account.
  • Support requests and communications.

1.2 Content You Upload or Provide

  • Media files you select for publishing (e.g., photos/videos) and metadata you provide (titles, captions, descriptions).

1.3 Information from Third-Party Platforms (e.g., TikTok)

  • Identifiers such as open_id (or similar account identifiers).
  • OAuth tokens (access token and refresh token) and expiry metadata.
  • Posting status information returned by platform APIs (e.g., publish_id, processing status, error codes).

1.4 Technical and Usage Data

  • Device/browser information, IP address, timestamps, logs, and analytics events.

2. How We Use Information

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the Service.
  • Authenticate you and manage connected third-party accounts.
  • Publish content to third-party platforms at your direction and provide status tracking.
  • Improve and secure the Service, prevent abuse, and debug issues.
  • Communicate with you about updates and support.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

3. How We Share Information

3.1 With Third-Party Platforms You Connect

When you instruct us to publish content, we transmit your content and required metadata to the connected platform (e.g., TikTok) using the permissions you granted.

3.2 Service Providers

We may share information with vendors who help us operate the Service (e.g., hosting, storage/CDN, analytics) under contractual confidentiality and security obligations.

3.3 Legal and Safety

We may disclose information if required by law or to protect rights, safety, and security.

3.4 Business Transfers

If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

4. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to provide the Service, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements.

Posting media retention: Media used for publishing may be stored temporarily to complete publishing and status tracking, then deleted after a reasonable period (e.g., 7–30 days), unless you choose to keep them for reuse or longer retention is required by law.

5. Security

We use reasonable safeguards to protect information, but no method of transmission or storage is 100% secure.

6. Your Choices and Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete your information and withdraw consent where applicable.
